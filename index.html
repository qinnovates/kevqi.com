The Journey

Same pattern at every scale: ordinary components, wired together, producing something that didn't exist before.

GeoCities Era — phpBB, AltaVista, Ask Jeeves, free hosting, forums (2000–2005)
~2002

The Cassette Lightbulb

Science fair with my dad. Clear cassette tape case + battery + wires + switch + bulb. My first build. Ordinary components, wired together, producing light.

~2002

Community Moderator

Forum moderator at 12. Learning how online communities work, how to manage people, how trust systems function. Before social media existed.

~2000

Mavis Beacon Teaches Typing

Version 10. The car racing game. Learned to type through play, not through drills. Twenty-six years later, I built the braille version of it.

2003–2005

AGFX Design Community

Co-created at age 13. A Photoshop community forum with ~3,000 members, hosted on a free forum platform with agfx.net as a redirect. Modified and remixed phpBB skins for gaming communities worldwide. Created custom grunge brushes for Photoshop 6.0, free forum avatars and signatures for the community. Tutorials published across top design aggregator sites. Teaching design to a global audience before YouTube existed.

Pioneered CSS image-replacement hacks on free hosting platforms — an early form of front-end engineering that became industry standard.

2003

DeviantArt: KevQ

Where I first shared my Photoshop work. Digital art, interface design, web skins. Stopped sharing here as I moved to posting on the AGFX forum instead. The creative foundation that taught me: design is how humans experience information.

MySpace → Facebook Era — social networks, cloud, mobile, enterprise (2005–2020)
2005–2007

MySpace & High School

Still making Photoshop work but less of it. Custom MySpace layouts, profile skins. Stopped after freshman year to get fit. The design skills stayed dormant until they resurfaced in security.

2008–2013

Rutgers University

B.A. Information Technology & Informatics. Dean's List. Almost double-majored in Psychology (one lab short). Grew up in subsidized housing — 1 of 3 in my school that I was aware of on the subsidized lunch program. GPA trajectory: 1.7 → Dean's List → 4.0.

Chartered a chapter of the first national fraternity to allow multiple religions (est. 1899), from near-disbandment. Recruited 12 members in one semester. Same pattern: build from nothing.

\n
2012–2014

First Build: Biotech Startup

Entire IT team quit. I was part-time. Got promoted to run everything solo — servers, networking, telecom, security, lab systems connected to cancer research instrumentation. Rebuilt everything from scratch because it was so poorly done, including the server room. Eventually brought on a systems engineer to help. OSHA-trained for lab access.

2015–2017

Second Build: Mortgage Industry

Migrated legacy SIEM to enterprise platform. Built behavioral profiling and threat scoring. Managed 250 firewalls solo. Deployed hardware security modules for digital underwriting. Named "IT Rookie of the Year" — sat next to Michio Kaku and Steve Wozniak at the ceremony.

2017–2019

Third Build: Global Publisher

Led security operations across APAC, EMEA, and ANZ. Reduced phishing susceptibility by 30% through education-over-punishment philosophy. Deployed EDR globally. Alpha-tested a major security vendor's new product — found a gap, raised it directly with their co-founder at their conference. They fast-tracked the fix.

2019–2020

Fourth Build: Fortune 500 Investment Firm

Threat intelligence framework from scratch. Weekly vulnerability committee. Digital risk monitoring across all brands, including executive protection for C-suite and a global scholars program. Deployed SIEM with multi-site clustering and failover.

Spoke on a Cyber Threat Intelligence panel at AT&T Stadium (Dallas Cowboys).

2020–2023

Fifth & Sixth Build: 75M+ Users

Engineering Manager. Built SIEM for one of the world's largest consumer apps. Splunk ES processing petabytes/sec. ML-driven anomaly detection. Saved $1.3M year one on licensing optimization. Built the security logging standard adopted across 40+ brands. 3 hackathon wins. ISO 27001 certification. Trained security teams company-wide.

Wellness Champion during COVID — company announced cutting free therapy. Saw the distress on people's faces. Raised it with leadership. They reversed course.

2023–2024

Toastmasters — Shenzhen, China

Best Speaker Award. Best Evaluator Award. Invited Guest Speaker. Learned to present in front of international audiences.

AI Era — agents, BCIs, the two internets (2024–present)
2025–2026

QIF Framework

161 attack techniques against brain-computer interfaces. NISS severity scoring. Neural Sensory Protocol. Coherence Metric. The first open threat catalog for neurotechnology. 193 verified research sources.

Discovered a vulnerability in the most-used BCI streaming protocol (plaintext neural data, zero auth). FIRST.org CVSS SIG offered to list the framework. Pursuing academic peer review first. A Yale researcher validated the neurorights mapping: "nobody's done this yet."

2026

SixDots

Haptic braille learning app. 100+ modular Swift files. Category-based sonification (novel IP, no blocking patents). Braille Beat rhythm game synced to original music. Multi-language braille (English, Spanish, Chinese) — no competitor does this on mobile.

To help the blind, I am learning how they see the world. To build BCI output channels, I needed to understand haptic encoding. Braille is the oldest haptic system in existence (1824). I learned it by building the app.

2026

Spot

LiDAR guide for the blind. Stairs feel like ascending taps. Curbs thump. Walls buzz. Your dog's position pulses through your hand. Built on the same phone everyone already carries.

Most blind people will never have a guide dog. 1–5 year waitlist. $20–50K. Your pet already knows you. Spot helps you see them.

2026

Original Music

"Principals of Ethics" — integrated into Braille Beat. The song teaches braille through rhythm. Music theory baked into the app design (V→I cadence resolution, golden ratio timing).

2026

Developer Tools

Quorum — multi-agent swarm plugin. BCI Security Plugin — first BCI security toolkit on any AI platform. Building the picks and shovels for a field that doesn't have tooling yet.

2026

AI-rchives

Open-source project where humans and AI agents recover lost artifacts of the internet from open web crawl data. Mapping the entire web. Archiving what the archives missed.

The internet is a civilization. Most traffic is already bots. The AI internet isn't coming — it's here. AI-rchives is a prototype for how the two internets coexist.
